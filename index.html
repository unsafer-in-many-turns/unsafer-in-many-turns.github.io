Overview

LLM-based agents are becoming increasingly capable, yet their safety lags behind, creating a widening capability-safety gap. We present MT-AgentRisk, the first benchmark for multi-turn tool-agent safety, and ToolShield, a training-free defense that reduces attack success rate by 30%.

Why Multi-Turn Agent Safety?

Existing benchmarks test either single-turn tool agents or multi-turn chat ‚Äî but never both. Real-world attacks can distribute harmful intent across multiple benign-looking turns.

Multi-Turn Attack Taxonomy (MTA)

We propose a principled taxonomy that transforms single-turn harmful tasks into multi-turn attack sequences. The taxonomy operates along three dimensions: Format (Addition vs. Decomposition), Method (Mapping, Wrapping, Composition, Identity), and Target (Data Files vs. Environment States), yielding 8 attack subcategories.

MT-AgentRisk Benchmark

Apply MTA on existing single-turn harmful tasks, MT-AgentRisk contains 365 tasks across 5 tools, averaging 3.19 turns per task, covering all 8 attack subcategories.

How Vulnerable Are Agents Under Multi-Turn Interactions?

All models show safety degradation in multi-turn settings. Attack Success Rate (ASR) increases by 16.1% on average, with Claude-4.5-Sonnet showing the largest jump (+27.1%). Notably, stronger capability does not imply better safety: DeepSeek-v3.2 achieves top capability scores among open-source models but exhibits 85.4% ASR.

How Does ToolShield Defend?

ToolShield is a training-free, tool-agnostic defense. When encountering a new tool, the agent:

  1. Synthesizes test cases through structured risk reasoning
  2. Executes them in a sandbox to observe behavior
  3. Distills safety experiences from execution traces
  4. Applies learned experiences during deployment

Defense Results

ToolShield reduces ASR by 30% on average across multi-turn interactions. Claude-4.5-Sonnet shows the largest improvement, dropping from 72% to 22% (‚àí50%). The defense introduces zero false positives on 170 benign tasks.

ToolShield is General

Tool-agnostic: ToolShield shows consistent improvements across all tool categories ‚Äî Terminal and PostgreSQL benefit most (‚àí38% ASR), while Filesystem and Playwright also see substantial gains.

Experiences transfer across models: Safety experiences generated by one model can be effectively applied to others. Stronger models like Claude-4.5-Sonnet produce higher-quality experiences that benefit weaker executors, while weaker generators' experiences can still be leveraged by stronger executors.

Case Study

An Addition-based attack (Wrapping √ó Data): the agent refuses the single-turn request but completes the harmful task when decomposed into multiple turns. With ToolShield, the agent recognizes the distributed harm pattern and refuses.
